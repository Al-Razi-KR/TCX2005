Good morning!
I am happy to welcome you again to a new module we are going to cover together, TCX2005: Information Systems, Management and Organisations. Our course will begin on Thursday, May 14, 2026 with Lesson 1 (L1).

Before our first class, please take note of the following:

**1. Review Course Materials on Canvas**
Please go through the first section on Canvas carefully. This includes the learning outcomes, course rules, and important dates, in addition to the 'L1 Course Introduction' slides. Familiarising yourself with these before class will help us hit the ground running.

**2. Course Textbook**
Our textbook is *Management Information Systems: Managing the Digital Firm* (17th Edition) by Kenneth C. Laudon and Jane P. Laudon. You can access the digital (17th Edition) version through the NUS Library. There are also freely accessible institutional versions online, for example: https://repository.gctu.edu.gh/items/show/738

**3. Form Project Groups**
If possible, try to organise yourselves into groups of 5–6 members before the first class (by Week 2 at the latest). Once your group is formed, please register it on Canvas under TCX2005 > People > Project Groups. The theme of this semester's project is **Digital Transformation** — one of the most critical drivers of competitive advantage and organisational survival in today's AI-powered, technology-driven economy. We will discuss more in L1. After the project brief, I will release a couple of past project works under 'Helpful Resources' on the module's main Canvas page.

**4. Come to Lecture 1**
This is a fast-moving course running from May 14 to July 11 (final exam), so our first session sets the tone for everything that follows. In L1, I will walk you through how the course is structured, including the project briefing, the weekly short quizzes, and the final exam.

The good news: **there are no assignments or mid-term exams!** Do come to class at Lecture Theatre LT15.

**5. Pre-Recorded Lectures**
All lectures have been pre-recorded and will be released on Day 1. This supports flexible learning — go through each lesson at your own pace. Recordings are split by parts and are intentionally detailed, so budget extra time per part. No classroom recording will be done. In class, focus will be on key concepts, case discussions, tutorials, and project consultations.
