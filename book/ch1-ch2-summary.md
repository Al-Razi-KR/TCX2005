## Chapter 1 — Information Systems in Global Business Today

### How IS Is Transforming Business
Global IT spending hit **$3.8 trillion** in 2019. Five forces reshaping business:
1. IT innovations (cloud, mobile, big data, IoT, ML, social networks)
2. New business models (Netflix, Apple iTunes)
3. E-commerce expansion ($3.6T in 2019 → projected $5T by 2021)
4. Management changes (always-on mobile, real-time enterprise data)
5. Organizational changes (competency over hierarchy, faster decisions)

**Globalization**: Internet reduced global communication costs to near zero, enabling "flattened world" operations.

**Digital Firm**: nearly all significant business relationships with customers, suppliers, and employees are digitally enabled. Time-shifting (24/7) and space-shifting (work from anywhere) are core traits. Examples approaching full digital status: Cisco, 3M, GE.

### Six Strategic Business Objectives of IS
| Objective | Example |
|---|---|
| Operational Excellence | Walmart's Retail Link → $524B sales (2019) |
| New Products/Services/Models | Apple iPhone/iTunes |
| Customer & Supplier Intimacy | Mandarin Oriental tracks guest preferences |
| Improved Decision Making | Real-time data replaces "information fog" |
| Competitive Advantage | Doing things better/cheaper than rivals |
| Survival | ATMs became industry necessity after Barclays introduced them in 1967 |

### What Is an Information System?

**Data** → raw facts (bar codes at checkout)  
**Information** → data shaped into useful form (total sales by region)

**Three core activities:**
- **Input** — captures raw data
- **Processing** — converts to meaningful form
- **Output** — delivers to users
- **Feedback** — output returned to correct/evaluate input

### Three Dimensions of IS

**Organizations**: hierarchy (senior → middle → operational mgmt), functional areas (sales, manufacturing, finance, HR), and unique **culture** embedded in their systems.

**Management**: senses situations, makes decisions, formulates action plans, designs new products.

**IT**: hardware, software, data management, networking/telecom, Internet, intranets, extranets — together forming the **IT infrastructure**.

### Complementary Assets
IT investment alone doesn't guarantee returns. You also need:
- **Organizational**: supportive culture, efficient processes, decentralized authority
- **Managerial**: senior support, teamwork culture, training
- **Social**: Internet infrastructure, education systems, laws/regulations

### IS as a Multidisciplinary Field (Sociotechnical View)
Optimal performance requires jointly optimizing both social and technical systems — technology must fit people and organizations, and organizations must also adapt to technology.

## Chapter 2 — Global E-business and Collaboration

### Business Processes and IS

**Business process**: organized, coordinated set of activities producing a product or service, supported by flows of material, information, and knowledge.

**Sample functional processes:**
| Function | Processes |
|---|---|
| Manufacturing | Assemble product, check quality, produce BOM |
| Sales & Marketing | Identify customers, advertise, sell |
| Finance & Accounting | Pay creditors, create financial statements |
| Human Resources | Hire, evaluate performance, enroll in benefits |

**Cross-functional processes** (e.g., fulfilling a customer order) span Sales → Accounting → Manufacturing → Logistics.

IT improves processes by: automating steps, enabling parallel tasks, allowing simultaneous information access, and enabling entirely new business models.

---

### Systems for Different Management Groups

**Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)**
- Serve **operational management**
- Record daily routine transactions (payroll, order entry, hotel reservations)
- Critical — failure even for hours is catastrophic

**Management Information Systems (MIS)**
- Serve **middle management**
- Summarize TPS data into scheduled routine reports (sales by product/region)
- Limited analytics — comparisons and simple summaries

**Decision-Support Systems (DSS)**
- Handle unique, rapidly changing problems with no predefined procedure
- Pull in external data (stock prices, competitor pricing)
- Example: shipping company voyage-estimating DSS for optimal vessel/speed/load

**Executive Support Systems (ESS)**
- Serve **senior management** on strategic, long-horizon decisions
- Deliver dashboards and graphs through easy portals
- Example: Valero's Refining Dashboard (real-time plant performance vs. plan)

### Enterprise Applications

| Application | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **ERP** (Enterprise Resource Planning) | Integrates manufacturing, finance, sales, HR into one system with shared data repository |
| **SCM** (Supply Chain Management) | Manages supplier relationships; shares orders, inventory, delivery data |
| **CRM** (Customer Relationship Management) | Coordinates all customer-facing processes to maximize revenue and satisfaction |
| **KMS** (Knowledge Management) | Captures and applies firm-wide expertise and knowledge |

**Intranets**: internal company networks (employees only)  
**Extranets**: extend intranet access to authorized vendors/suppliers  
**E-commerce**: buying/selling over the Internet (subset of e-business)  
**E-government**: Internet-enabled government-citizen and government-business relationships

### Collaboration and Social Business

**Collaboration**: working with others toward shared explicit goals.

**Why it matters more than ever:**
1. 41% of US jobs are interaction-primary (knowledge work)
2. Professional/service sector growth demands information sharing
3. Work organized in teams, expertise pushed down
4. Global firms require globally coordinating teams (Ford: 199K people, 61 plants)
5. Innovation is inherently a group/social process
6. Diverse teams outperform individuals

**Social business**: using social networking platforms (Facebook, Twitter, Yammer) to engage employees, customers, and suppliers — includes crowdsourcing, shared workspaces, wikis, communities.

**Business benefits:**
| Benefit | Why |
|---|---|
| Productivity | Expert knowledge captured; problems solved faster |
| Quality | Errors surface and spread faster |
| Innovation | More diverse ideas |
| Customer service | Complaints resolved faster |
| Financial performance | Better sales, growth, profitability |

**Time/Space Collaboration Matrix:**
|  | Same Place | Different Place |
|---|---|---|
| **Same Time** | Face-to-face, decision rooms | Videoconferencing, instant messaging |
| **Different Time** | Shift work groupware | Email, wikis, blogs, async conferencing |

**Major platforms**: Google Workspace, Microsoft SharePoint, IBM Notes, Salesforce Chatter, Microsoft Yammer, Facebook Workplace

### The IS Function in Organizations

**IS Department** maintains hardware, software, data, and networks.

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Programmers** | Write software instructions |
| **Systems analysts** | Translate business problems into IT requirements; liaison role |
| **IS managers** | Lead teams, manage projects and operations |
| **CIO** | Oversees IT use enterprise-wide |
| **CSO** | IS security policy |
| **CPO** | Data privacy compliance |
| **CKO** | Knowledge management |
| **CDO** | Enterprise-wide data governance |

**IT Governance**: strategy and policies governing IT use — defines decision rights and accountability to ensure IT supports organizational strategy.
